We are both going into freshman year, and Snapchat is already a huge part of how people communicate. Most kids in our grade already have it, including a lot of people on our future football team. Group chats, plans, workouts, and random conversations already happen there every day, and right now we are kind of outside of all of it.

This summer we are starting football training, weights, and getting to know people before high school starts. A lot of communication already happens through Snapchat group chats because it is quick and easy. It is not really about popularity. It is more about being included and connected.

Addressing Concerns
Safety Has To Be
Part Of The Deal
We understand why parents worry about social media. There are real concerns about talking to strangers, sending things you should not, or people taking advantage of kids online. Honestly, we get that.
Family Center
Parents can see who their teenager is talking to without reading private messages.
Friend Settings
Only people we add back can normally contact us or view our stories.
No Random Strangers
We are not adding random strangers or meeting up with people online.
Screenshot Alerts
If somebody screenshots something, Snapchat notifies you right away.
The Real Conversation
Trust Is Earned.
We Know That.
We are not asking for unlimited freedom or zero rules. We are completely fine with parents using Family Center, checking our accounts, or setting rules if that is what makes them comfortable.
Agreed Rules
We are completely okay with reasonable rules, limits, and check-ins.
No Secrets
Nothing about this needs to be secretive or hidden.
Track Record
We have already handled sports, school, and responsibilities seriously.
Final Argument
This is not really about getting another app. It is about being part of the way people our age already communicate and stay connected.
We understand there are real concerns about social media and online safety. We take those concerns seriously too. We just hope this shows we have actually thought about this and are asking for the chance to use Snapchat responsibly.
